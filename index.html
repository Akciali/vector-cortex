CORTEX · Topologies of Thought ✦

A living map of an AI's memory — explore it with your hands, in front of your webcam.

🔒 Your camera never leaves this device

Hand tracking runs entirely in your browser (Google's MediaPipe model + WebAssembly). No image, video, or camera frame is ever uploaded, stored, or sent anywhere — there is no server-side vision at all. This page has no backend: the demo graph is a static file and search runs entirely in your browser. Nothing about you is collected.

How it works
  • In-browser vision. MediaPipe Hands reads 21 landmarks per hand from the webcam, on-device. Your fingertip becomes the pointer; finger postures are the commands.
  • Synthetic memory. The 260 points are invented demo notes projected into a map. There is no database and no live memory behind this — a frozen, self-contained snapshot.
  • Gestures. Open hand points · fist grabs & drags · two fists zoom · a quick pinch targets a memory. One hand = centralized, two hands = distributed.
  • Client-side by design. Detection, rendering, and search are 100% JavaScript in your tab. Nothing touches a server — the whole demo is a static page.
